3
Find the mistake — there is ONE error in each sentence. Find it, then click to check. Error correction
"Sorry to interrupt, but could I just add something on here?"
Remove "on" → "Could I just add something here?" — "add something on" is not a natural combination.
"I see your view, but I'm not sure I agree with the proposal."
"view" → "I see your point…" — the fixed phrase is always "I see your point."
"Sorry, you're cutting up — could you check your microphone?"
"cutting" → "you're breaking up" — "breaking up" is the correct phrasal verb for a bad connection.
"Could you explain on that point a bit more, please?"
"explain" → "Could you elaborate on that?" — "elaborate on" is the correct phrase. You don't say "explain on."

Part 4 · Guided Speaking
Scenario: The Product Launch Call
15 min
Your role
Ana — Marketing team member
Chair
Mark
Goal
Agree on launch date for expense-tracking app
Situation 1 of 7
1 Joining the meeting
Ana just joined the video call. There are five attendees today. She's a little early — two colleagues are already there — and she's been asked to take minutes during the call.
💬 What does Ana say when she joins?
💡 Think about: greeting everyone + audio check
Sample answer
"Hi everyone, good morning! Can you hear me okay? Great — really looking forward to today's call."
2 Introducing the agenda
Mark says: "Right, let's get started — we have a lot to cover." He asks Ana, who sent out the agenda yesterday, to open the meeting and walk everyone through the items.
💬 How does Ana introduce the agenda?
💡 Use: "The purpose of today's meeting is…" + list 3 items
Sample answer
"Thanks, Mark. The purpose of today's meeting is to agree on the launch date for our new app and assign tasks. We have three items on the agenda: the timeline, the marketing plan, and task allocation."
3 Interrupting politely
A colleague, Pedro, has been talking about app features for a while. Ana has an important point about the launch date.
💬 How does Ana interrupt politely?
💡 Use: "Sorry to interrupt, but…" / "If I could just come in here…"
Sample answer
"Sorry to interrupt, Pedro, but I'd like to raise a point about the launch date — I think it affects everything else we're planning."
4 Disagreeing politely
Mark suggests launching on March 1st. Ana believes this is too early — the marketing campaign needs two more weeks. The team needs to reach a consensus before the call ends.
💬 How does Ana disagree — politely but clearly?
💡 Use: "I see your point, but…" / "Have you considered…?"
Sample answer
"I see your point, Mark, and I understand the pressure — but have you considered that the marketing campaign still needs two more weeks? March 15th would be more realistic."
5 Asking for clarification
Pedro says: "We need to make sure the UX flow is optimized before go-live." Ana isn't clear what "UX flow" means here.
💬 How does Ana ask for clarification without sounding rude?
💡 Use: "Could you elaborate on that?" / "What exactly do you mean by…?"
Sample answer
"Sorry Pedro, could you elaborate on 'UX flow'? I want to understand the scope before we assign tasks."
6 Technical issue
Mark starts speaking but no one can hear him. It seems he's on mute.
💬 What does Ana say?
💡 Keep it polite and clear — he may not hear you either!
Sample answer
"Mark, I think you might be on mute — we can't hear you. Could you check your microphone?"
7 Closing the meeting
The meeting is nearly over. Mark asks Ana to close by summarizing what was agreed and the next steps.
💬 How does Ana close the meeting professionally?
💡 Include: summary + action items + what you'll send & when
Sample answer
"Okay, let's wrap up. To summarize: we've agreed on March 15th as the launch date. Pedro will finalize the UX review by March 8th, and I'll complete the marketing plan by March 10th. The pricing question we'll table for next week. I'll circulate the minutes and action items to all attendees by end of day — and I'll loop in the legal team too. With that, I'd like to adjourn the meeting. Thanks everyone!"
